<p><strong>What is Cyber Wellness?</strong>
</p>
<p>Cyber Wellness is the ability of our children and youth to navigate the
cyberspace safely.</p>
<p>This is done through our <strong>Character and Citizenship Education (CCE) curriculum</strong> which
aims to:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>equip students with the knowledge and skills to harness the power of Information
and Communication Technology (ICT) for positive purposes.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>maintain a positive presence in cyberspace; and</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>be safe and responsible users of ICT.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Through the curriculum, your child learns knowledge and skills to have
balanced screen use, be safe online, and exhibit positive online behaviours.</p>

<p><strong>School Programmes&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Safer Internet Day&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>The school commemorated Safer Internet Day 2026 in conjunction with Total
Defence Day. The national theme for Safer Internet Day 2026 is <strong><em>“</em>Practise Healthy Digital Habits Together<em>”.</em></strong>
</p>
<p>The theme encourages students to practice the 4 digital habits:&nbsp;</p>
<p>a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Set </strong>boundaries to manage
screen use and online interactions</p>
<p>b.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Think </strong>before you act
to safeguard digital footprint</p>
<p>c.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Report </strong>inappropriate
content to keep everyone safe</p>
<p>d.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Engage </strong>a trusted person
and seek support proactively&nbsp;</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Cyber Wellness Day&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>WGPS’ Cyber Wellness Day is a dedicated effort to nurture safe, responsible,
and confident digital citizens. Through an engaging assembly programme
and thoughtfully designed classroom lessons, students learn the importance
of cybersecurity and how to protect themselves in the online world.&nbsp;</p>
<p>They are equipped with practical strategies to stay safe such as avoiding
interactions with strangers, recognising fake links and scams, and safeguarding
personal information. Students also learn to identify phishing attempts
and deepfake content, practicing the habit of “Stop &amp; Check” before
clicking or sharing. Importantly, they are encouraged to seek help from
trusted adults whenever they feel unsure, reinforcing a supportive environment
for navigating the digital space safely.</p>
